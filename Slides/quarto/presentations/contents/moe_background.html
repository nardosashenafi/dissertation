Switching Control with Deep-net Mixture of Experts

Background

  • Contact Modeling with Linear Complementarity Formulation
  • Mixture of Experts

Contact Modeling

  • Objective: accurately model contacts, impacts and Coulomb friction.
  • E.g. Model of bouncing ball


\[\begin{align*} g_N &= y \\ \dot{g}_N &= \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}}_{W_N} \begin{bmatrix} \dot{x} \\ \dot{y} \end{bmatrix} =: \gamma_N \end{align*}\]

\[ \gamma_N^+ = -\epsilon_N \gamma_N^- \]

  • Complementarity Condition \[ 0 \leq \xi_N \perp \lambda_N \geq 0 \]

Moreau’s Time-Stepping

  • Check if \(g_N \leq 0\) at \(t + \frac{\Delta t}{2}\)
  • Solve complementarity

\[\begin{align*} \begin{bmatrix} m & 0 \\ 0 & m \end{bmatrix} &(\dot{q}^+ - \dot{q}^-) - W_N \lambda_N - \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ mg \end{bmatrix} \Delta t = 0 \end{align*}\]

\[\begin{align*} \dot{q}^+ &= \begin{bmatrix} m & 0 \\ 0 & m \end{bmatrix}^{-1} \left[W_N \lambda_N + \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ mg \end{bmatrix} \Delta t \right] + \dot{q}^- \end{align*}\]

\[\begin{align*} \xi_N = W_N\dot{q}^+ + \epsilon_N W_N \dot{q}^- \end{align*}\]

\[\begin{align*} \xi_N = W_N\begin{bmatrix} m & 0 \\ 0 & m \end{bmatrix}^{-1} \left[W_N \lambda_N + \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ mg \end{bmatrix} \Delta t \right] + (1 + \epsilon_N) W_N \dot{q}^- \end{align*}\]

Complementarity condition \[\begin{align*} \xi_N \lambda_N = 0, \; \xi_N \geq 0, \lambda_N \geq 0 \end{align*}\]

  • For non-convex optimization, use Lemke’s algorithm

Mixture of Experts (MOE): Old Faithful

  • Objective: learn two expert Gaussian models to predict duration given waiting time
  • The experts are chosen as \[ F(x;\theta) = \{\mathcal{N}_1(\mu_1, \sigma_1), \mathcal{N}_2(\mu_2, \sigma_2)\} \]
  • The gating network is a neural net \(\mathbf{P}(x | \psi) = [P_1(x | \psi), P_2(x | \psi) ]\)
  • Learn the parameters \(\theta = \{(\mu_1, \sigma_1), (\mu_2, \sigma_2) \}\) and \(\psi\) from dataset \(\mathbb{D} = \{(x_1, y_1), \dots, (x_N, y_N) \}\)

  • Expectation Maximization: maximizes log likelihood \[ \ln \{P(\mathbb{D} | \theta, \psi) \} = \sum_{j=1}^{N} \ln \sum_{i=1}^{N_F} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2 \pi \sigma_i^2}} \exp \left( -\frac{1}{2}\frac{(y_j - \mu_i)^2}{\sigma_i^2} \right) P_i(x_j, \psi) \]

Generalization of the log likelihood

  • The experts can take many forms. The likelihood can be generalized as

\[ \ln \{P(\mathbb{D} | \theta, \psi) \} = \sum_{j=1}^{N} \ln \sum_{i=1}^{N_F} \mathcal{N} ( \| F_i(x_j; \theta_i) - y_j \| \; | \; 0, s) P_i(x_j, \psi), \]

\[ \ln \{P(\mathbb{D} | \theta, \psi) \} = \sum_{j=1}^{N} \ln \sum_{i=1}^{N_F} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2 \pi s^2}} \exp \left( -\frac{1}{2}\frac{ \|F_i(x_j; \theta_i) - y_j \|^2}{s^2} \right) P_i(x_j, \psi), \]

  • For gradient-based techniques, we can extract the relevant parts and simplify the likelihood \[ \ln \{P(\mathbb{D} | \theta, \psi) \} \propto \mathbb{L}(\mathbb{D} | \theta, \psi) = \sum_{j=1}^{N} \sum_{i=1}^{N_F} - \| F_i(x_j; \theta_i) - y_j \|^2 P_i(x_j | \psi). \]
Likelihood

\[ \mathbb{L}(\mathbb{D} | \theta, \psi) = \sum_{j=1}^{N} \sum_{i=1}^{N_F} - \| F_i(x_j; \theta_i) - y_j \|^2 P_i(x_j | \psi). \]
