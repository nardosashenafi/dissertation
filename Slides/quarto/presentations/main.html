Hybrid Systems

  • exhibit both continuous state flow and discrete state transitions
  • E.g. Room temperature control, contact-rich robots
  • We tackle two main challenges in the control of contact-rich robots.

1. Motivation

  • Switching control does not scale well
    • E.g. Cooperative manipulation
    • kk contacts can have upto 2k2^k contact combinations
  • It is difficult to parameterize the switching condition
  • Contacts cause mode changes
  • Each mode has distinct dynamics
  • Controlled by switching among several policies


1. Proposed Method



  • Our method uses data-driven technique to automatically learn a mixture of expert controller and the switching conditionals


  • We infer contact-aware controllers by training on trajectories generated from contact models
    • Contact-aware controllers minimize the adverse effects of contacts or leverage the potential contacts to their advantage

Mixture of Expert Controller


  • Objective: learn contact-aware mixture of expert controller and a gating network
  • Two major modifications to the old faithful example
    • training dataset is generated from trajectories
    • prediction error is replaced by running cost ℓ\ell of the trajectories
  • We pose the search over the parameters ψ,θ\psi, \theta as the following optimization problem

Optimization Problem

minimizeψ,θ∫0Tℓ(x(t),u)ⅆt,subject toM(q)ⅆq̇+h(x;ψ,θ)ⅆt−ⅆR=0,u={Fi(x;θi)|i∼Categorical(𝐏(x|ψ))}.\begin{align*} \begin{aligned} \underset{\psi, \theta}{\textrm{minimize}} & & & \int_0^T \ell (x(t),u) \dd t , \\% \textrm{subject to} & & & M(q) \dd \dot{q} + h(x; \psi, \theta)\dd t - \dd R = 0,\\% & & & u = \{F_i(x; \theta_i) \; | \; i \sim \text{Categorical} (\mathbf{P}(x| \psi)) \}. \end{aligned} \end{align*}

Training


  • Training procedure:
    • Start from initial parameters (ψ,θ)(\psi, \theta)
    • Sample initial state x0x_0
    • Generate trajectories ϕ(x0,u,T)\phi(x_0, u, T) using current parameters i∼Categorical(𝐏(x|ψ))u(x;ψ,θ)=Fi(x;θi)M(q)ⅆq̇+h(x;ψ,θ)ⅆt−ⅆR=0 (Moreau’s time stepping)\begin{gather*} i \sim \text{Categorical} (\mathbf{P}(x| \psi)) \\ u(x; \psi, \theta) = F_i(x; \theta_i) \\ M(q) \dd \dot{q} + h(x; \psi, \theta) \dd t - \dd R = 0 \text{ (Moreau's time stepping)} \end{gather*}
    • Assign a running cost ℓ\ell to the trajectories based on performance
    • Update parameters (ψ,θ)(\psi, \theta) to minimize running cost

Performance Objective


  1. Minimum Loss Trajectory (MTL):
  • Accumulated loss may not reflect desired behavior. E.g. Simple pendulum
  • Simple pendulum needs to pump slowly, which would accumulate large cost
  • MTL encourages trajectories to eventually lead to a minimum cost

Dissipativity


A dynamical system

Σ:{ẋ=f(x,u)y=g(x,u)x∈𝒳⊂ℝ2m,u∈𝒰⊂ℝn \Sigma: \quad \begin{cases} \dot{x} &= f(x,u) \\ y &= g(x,u) \end{cases} \quad x \in \mathcal{X} \subset \mathbb{R}^{2m}, \, u \in \mathcal{U} \subset \mathbb{R}^{n}

is dissipative with respect to some supply rate ss if there exists a storage function H:𝒳→ℝ+H: \mathcal{X} \to \mathbb{R}^{+} such that

H(x(t1))≤H(x(t0))+∫t0t1s(u(t),y(t))dt H\left( x(t_1) \right) \leq H\left( x(t_0) \right) + \int_{t_0}^{t_1} s(u(t), y(t)) \, \text{d}t

for all x(t0)=x0x(t_0) = x_0, all input uu, and all t1≥t0t_1 \geq t_0

Stability of Passive Systems


Σ:{ẋ=f(x,u),f(0,0)=0,y=g(x,u),g(0,0)=0, \Sigma: \quad \begin{cases} \dot{x} &= f(x,u), && f(0,0) = 0, \\ y &= g(x,u), && g(0,0) = 0, \end{cases}

u⊤y≥Ḣ=∂H∂xf(x,u),H≥0,y≡0⟹x≡0.\begin{gather*} u^{\top} y \geq \dot{H} = \frac{\partial H}{\partial x} f(x,u), \\ \quad H \geq 0,\quad y \equiv 0 \implies x \equiv 0. \end{gather*}


Lemma (Khalil, 2002)

The origin of Σ\Sigma is:

  • stable if Σ\Sigma is passive,
  • asymptotically stable if Σ\Sigma is output strictly passive (s=u⊤y−δ∥y∥2,δ>0s = u^\top y - \delta \lVert y \rVert^2, \; \delta > 0).

Bayesian Learning


  • Objective: Given finite dataset with inherent noise, we are in search of a regression model
  • E.g. Recognizing handwritten digits from images
    • noise due to differences in individual handwriting
  • Stochastic models provide generalized predictions and report the uncertainty in each prediction
    • The model is given by F(x;θ)F(x;\theta) where the parameter θ\theta is a sample drawn from a distribution

  • Variational Inference: approximates the posterior P(θ|𝔻)P(\theta | \mathbb{D}) with a pre-selected distribution Q(θ;z)Q(\theta; z)
  • Objective: collect NθN_\theta samples from the current posterior Q(θ;z)Q(\theta; z) and maximize Elbo ℒ(𝔻,z)=𝔼θ∼Q[ln(P(𝔻∣θ;z)P(θ))−ln(Q(θ;z))]. \mathcal{L}(\mathbb{D},z) = \mathbb{E}_{\theta \sim Q} \left[\ln(P(\mathbb{D} \mid \theta;z)P(\theta)) - \ln(Q(\theta;z)) \right].

Robustness via Bayesian Learning



  • NeuralPbc assumes a nominal model ẋ=f(x,u)\dot{x} = f(x, u)
  • The trained controller must not overfit on the observations generated from the nominal model

  • Our method: HdH_d is a Bayesian neural network
    • achieves the performance objective ℓ\ell for samples θ∼Q(θ;z)\theta \sim Q(\theta; z)
    • searches for ensemble of parameters that meet the desired performance
